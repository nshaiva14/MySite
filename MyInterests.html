I am a cognitive science major with an eagerness to understand and analyze the complex processes that drive our everyday thoughts, behaviors, and decisions. The systems we use to represent, register, and transform information are highly elaborate, so cognitive scientists aim to integrate research from a diverse array of disciplines: computer science, philosophy, psychology, linguistics, and neuroscience to simplify, clarify, and analyze human cognitive processes. One major focus in the field pertains to artificial intelligence research. I believe that by investigating the inner-workings of machines, the computer can serve as a virtual mirror, reflecting our quintessential behaviors and tendencies. My desire to study cognitive science is reflective of my personality in many ways. I enjoy brainstorming innovative solutions to problems. I believe that with ambition and persistence, nothing is impossible. Instead of dealing with the abstract, a cognitive scientist uses the scientific method, a computational algorithm, or other concrete formalized approach to solve problems. Likewise, I strive to bring abstract thoughts to life with meticulous effort and a clear course of action. I am a thorough planner and skilled at juggling many responsibilities. My eagerness to understand and empathize with others has fueled my development of strong interpersonal skills. It gives me great joy to make others feel welcome and engaged.
